German Migration

Germany was a very accepting country until world war 1 broke out. During the 1830s, the Germans had been persecuted because of religious bias and different religious beliefs. In 1838, German migration started and many Germans left their homeland due to persecution, to start a new life in Australia where there was acceptance of all beliefs. They initially settled in Adelaide SA and moved around from there.

reasons for migration

Germany didn't always migrate due to persecution. Numerous germans migrated and still to this day migrate to australia because their desire to explore and learn with more economic development. The Jews before them, fled from the persecution to australia and gave germany an idea of what they can do.

Contributions

Some contributions the germans had made during their initial settlement includes sharing their culture and beliefs.
